This is a digital tool for practicing "Active Listening," turning a common therapy exercise into a tangible module.
 * How it Works: This is a 3-step, turn-based module.
 * Step 1 (Partner A): Partner A gets a prompt: "Share a low-stakes feeling or stressor from your day. (e.g., 'I am feeling overwhelmed by the laundry.')"
 * Step 2 (Partner B): Partner B cannot see Partner A's entry yet. They see a prompt: "Your partner is about to share a feeling. Your only job is to listen. You are not allowed to solve the problem, defend yourself, or state your own feeling."
 * Step 3 (The Exchange): Partner B now sees Partner A's entry. They must respond in two, distinct text boxes:
   * Echo: "What I hear you saying is..."
   * Empathy: "I can understand why you would feel..."
 * Why it Works: It forces the couple to practice the structure of validation. You can review the exchange and see who is struggling with the "solve" vs. "listen" part. The "IFS Introduction" (An Inner World Bridge)
This is perfect for your interest in Internal Family Systems (IFS) and "Inner Child" work.
 * How it Works: You assign an individual module to each partner (like your "Alchemical Grimoire" or "Shadow Work" app).
 * Therapist Assignment: "Complete the 'Meet Your Parts' exercise. Identify one 'Protective Part' that shows up in your relationship (e.g., 'The Angry Part,' 'The Silent Part')."
 * The "Bridge": A new module where each partner writes a short letter from that Part to their partner, explaining its job.
   * Example: "Dear Matthew, I am Carly's 'Critical Part.' I show up and criticize you about chores because I'm terrified she will be abandoned if she isn't 'perfect.' I'm not trying to hurt you; I'm trying to protect her."

This is a more advanced, "in-the-moment" app that can be part of your "Citadel" or "Aura" client-facing app.
 * How it Works: A "Pause" button is visible in the app. When a couple is arguing at home, either partner can press it.
 * The "Pause": Pressing the button sends a notification to both partners' phones. It triggers a shared 20-minute "cool down" timer.
 * During the Pause: The app locks them out of the argument. Instead, it directs them both to a simple, individual exercise, like your "Bonfire of Breath" app.
 * The "Resume": After 20 minutes, a prompt asks, "Are you ready to resume this conversation calmly? Here is the 'I Feel' Translator tool to help you start."
 * Why it Works: It digitizes the concept of a "physiological time-out" to stop emotional flooding, a core skill for high-conflict couples.
